This **Power BI dashboard** visualizes the results of a survey conducted by the **European Social Survey (ESS)** [ESS Official Website](https://www.europeansocialsurvey.org/), which collected responses from individuals across
European countries (published 09.04.18). The goal of this analysis is to understand public opinion on climate change, daily energy habits, and preferences for future electricity generation.

---

## Questions Answered
The dashboard was designed to answer the following questions:
1. **General opinion:** What is the general opinion of Europeans regarding climate change?
2. **Level of concern:** How worried are Europeans about climate change?
3. **Energy preferences:** What are the European preferences towards different energy sources (Coal, Solar, Nuclear, etc.)?
4. **Geographic differences:** How do these opinions and habits differ between various European countries?

---

## Dataset
The dataset comes from the **European Social Survey (ESS)** and the key variables analyzed in this project include:

**Everyday energy habits:**
* `eneffap`: Likelihood to buy the most energy-efficient home appliances.
* `rdcenr`: Frequency of doing things to reduce daily energy use.
* `cflsenr`: Confidence in the ability to use less energy than currently used.

**Preferences for electricity generation:**
Respondents were asked how much electricity in their country should be generated from:
* `elgcoal`: Coal
* `elgngas`: Natural Gas
* `elghydr`: Hydroelectric power
* `elgnuc`: Nuclear power
* `elgsun`: Solar power

## Key insights
Based on the survey data visualized in the dashboard, here are the main findings regarding European attitudes toward climate change and energy:

* **High awareness, moderate engagement:** An overwhelming majority (**91.75%**) of respondents perceive that the world's climate is changing. However, despite this high awareness, the average person only shows **"medium engagement"** when it comes to personal climate responsibility.
* **Perceived causes of climate change:** Most Europeans attribute climate change to human behavior to some degree. **45.91%** believe it is caused about equally by natural processes and human activity, while **38.68%** believe it is driven *mainly* by human activity. 
* **Levels of Concern:** The population is generally concerned but not panicked. The highest proportion of people are **"somewhat worried" (46.29%)** or "very worried" (22.71%), while extreme worry remains low (5.20%). 
* **Clear preference for renewable energy:** When asked about preferences for electricity generation, respondents showed a massive preference for green energy. **Solar and wind** power were the top choices for generating "a very large amount" of electricity. 
* **Rejection of fossil fuels and nuclear:** Conversely, **Coal and Nuclear** energy face strong public opposition, with these two sources overwhelmingly dominating the "None at all" category for desired electricity generation.

By filtering the dashboard to specifically analyze responses from Italy, several unique geographic trends emerge compared to the broader European average:

* **Stronger attribution to human activity:** While the European average leans toward believing climate change is caused "equally by natural and human processes," Italians diverge from this. The largest segment in Italy (**45.32%**) believes it is driven **"mainly by human activity."**
* **Concentrated moderate concern:** Over half of the Italian respondents (**52.46%**) report being "somewhat worried" about climate change. This is higher than the European average, showing a very concentrated, moderate level of anxiety, though extreme worry remains low (7.43%).
* **Overwhelming rejection of nuclear energy:** The energy preference chart for Italy shows a massive spike in opposition to nuclear power. Nearly **60%** of respondents chose "None at all" for nuclear energy, which is a stark contrast to other energy sources. Coal is also heavily rejected.
* **Solar is the clear favorite:** In terms of desired energy, **Solar power** is the undisputed favorite in Italy, with over 60% of people wanting "a very large amount" of it generated, closely followed by Wind power.
